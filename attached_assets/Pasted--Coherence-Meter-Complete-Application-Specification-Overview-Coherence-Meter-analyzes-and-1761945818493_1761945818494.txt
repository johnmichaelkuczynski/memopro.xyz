# Coherence Meter: Complete Application Specification

## Overview

Coherence Meter analyzes and improves text coherence across multiple dimensions. It evaluates whether text holds together at different levels (logical, thematic, instructional, etc.) and provides both diagnostic reports and rewrites that maximize the selected type of coherence.

**IMPORTANT PLACEMENT:** This function should be positioned ABOVE "GPT Bypass Humanizer" and BELOW "Text Model Validator" in the application interface.

---

## Core Concept

Coherence exists at multiple levels, from basic logical consistency to sophisticated thematic unity. Different text types require different kinds of coherence:
- Academic arguments need logical cohesiveness
- Fiction needs thematic/psychological coherence
- Management communications need instructional coherence
- Marketing/coaching content needs motivational coherence
- Scientific writing needs explanatory coherence that aligns with natural law

This tool identifies which type of coherence a text needs (or lets the user specify), evaluates how well the text achieves it, and provides a rewrite that maximizes that coherence type.

---

## The Seven Coherence Types

### Type 1: Logical Consistency (Non-Contradiction)
**Definition:** Text contains no direct logical contradictions. Statements don't contradict each other.

**When to use:**
- Most basic level of coherence
- Foundation for all other types
- Minimum requirement for any serious writing

**Example of failure:**
```
The ball is red. The ball is blue. The ball has no color.
```

**What the tool checks:**
- Direct contradictions (X and not-X)
- Incompatible claims
- Mutually exclusive statements presented as simultaneously true

---

### Type 2: Logical Cohesiveness (Argumentative Structure)
**Definition:** Claims don't just avoid contradictionâ€”they actively support each other in a directed way. Each statement builds on or follows from previous statements.

**When to use:**
- Philosophy papers
- Legal arguments
- Academic essays
- Proofs
- Policy documents

**Example of failure:**
```
Democracy is the best form of government. Apples contain vitamin C. 
Therefore, we should increase military spending.
```
(No contradiction, but claims don't support each other)

**Example of success:**
```
According to the computational theory of mind, to think is to compute. But what 
is meant by 'compute'? The generally given answer: computing is symbol manipulation 
driven exclusively by formal properties. However, 'formal' is ambiguous between 
syntactic and morphological senses. CTM fails on each disambiguation. Once we 
register this ambiguity, arguments for CTM cease to be compelling.
```
(Each claim builds on the previous; the argument has directed structure)

**What the tool checks:**
- Premise-conclusion relationships
- Whether intermediate steps are provided
- Whether evidence supports claims
- Whether the logical structure is valid
- Whether there are gaps in reasoning

---

### Type 3: Scientific/Explanatory Coherence
**Definition:** Explanations align with natural law and known mechanisms. The account could plausibly be true given how the world actually works.

**When to use:**
- Scientific writing
- Technical documentation
- Investigative journalism
- Medical/health content
- Any causal explanation

**Example of failure:**
```
Quantum entanglement allows particles to communicate faster than light. This proves 
consciousness creates reality because observers affect quantum states. Therefore, 
positive thinking can heal cancer by collapsing the wave function in your favor.
```

**What's wrong:**
- Entanglement does NOT allow FTL communication
- Observer effect â‰  consciousness
- No mechanism linking thoughts to tumor biology
- Ignores decoherence at biological scales

**Example of success:**
```
Quantum entanglement creates correlations between particles but does not allow 
faster-than-light communication. While measurements require interactions, these 
need not involve consciousnessâ€”any physical interaction suffices. At macroscopic 
biological scales, quantum effects are negligible due to thermal decoherence. 
Mental states can affect health through stress reduction and immune function via 
well-understood neuroendocrine pathways.
```

**What the tool checks:**
- Violations of physical laws
- Implausible causal mechanisms
- Missing explanatory steps
- Contradiction with established science
- Appropriate scope (quantum effects at macro scale = red flag)

---

### Type 4: Thematic/Psychological Cohesiveness
**Definition:** Mood, imagery, emotional trajectory, and psychological feel maintain consistency and flow naturally. The "texture" of the writing holds together.

**When to use:**
- Fiction
- Memoir
- Personal essays
- Creative nonfiction
- Literary journalism

**Example of failure:**
```
I came home from the funeral, devastated by my loss. The sun was shining brightly! 
I decided to reorganize my tax documents. My heart felt like it would never heal. 
I wondered what to have for lunchâ€”maybe tacos?
```

**What's wrong:**
- Emotional tone whipsaws (devastation â†’ cheerful â†’ mundane â†’ grief â†’ casual)
- Imagery doesn't support mood (bright sun during grief without acknowledging contrast)
- No psychological plausibility in the sequence

**Example of success:**
```
I came home from college. It was rainingâ€”not significant in itself, I know, but 
it certainly fit my mood. My father was home; I heard the familiar tinkling of ice 
in his tumbler; I knew that the abuse would start just a second later.
```

**What works:**
- Weather mirrors internal state
- Sensory details (tinkling ice) build dread
- Past experience informs present emotion
- Psychological progression is believable

**What the tool checks:**
- Consistency of emotional tone
- Whether imagery supports mood
- Psychological plausibility of reactions
- Flow of emotional trajectory
- Whether shifts are motivated or jarring

---

### Type 5: Instructional Coherence
**Definition:** Sends a consistent, actionable message. The reader knows exactly what they are supposed to do. No contradictory directives.

**When to use:**
- Management communications
- How-to guides
- Standard operating procedures
- Training materials
- User manuals

**Example of failure:**
```
To increase sales, focus on customer relationships. Make sure to close deals quickly. 
Spend time understanding client needs. Don't waste time on prospects who aren't 
ready to buy. Build long-term partnerships. Hit your monthly quota no matter what.
```

**What's wrong:**
- "Build relationships" vs "Close quickly" (quality vs speed)
- "Understand needs" vs "Don't waste time" (depth vs efficiency)
- "Long-term partnerships" vs "Hit quota no matter what" (sustainability vs pressure)
- Reader doesn't know which directive to prioritize

**Example of success:**
```
To increase sales, prioritize building genuine customer relationships. Take time to 
understand each client's specific needs, even if it means longer sales cycles. Focus 
on prospects who are ready to engage meaningfully rather than chasing quick wins. 
Cultivate long-term partnerships that will generate sustainable revenue. Your quota 
should be met through quality relationships, not rushed closings.
```

**What works:**
- Single consistent strategy (relationship-building)
- All directives support the same approach
- Clear prioritization
- No contradictory guidance

**What the tool checks:**
- Contradictory instructions
- Unclear priorities
- Ambiguous action items
- Whether directives support or undermine each other
- Whether reader can extract clear action plan

---

### Type 6: Motivational Coherence
**Definition:** User knows how they are supposed to feel. Emotional direction is clear and maintained throughout. Inspires consistent psychological state.

**When to use:**
- Self-help content
- Marketing copy
- Coaching materials
- Motivational speeches
- Team pep talks

**Example of failure:**
```
You can achieve anything you set your mind to! But be realistic about your limitations. 
Dream big and take massive action! Remember that most people fail, so manage your 
expectations. You are unstoppable! Just don't get your hopes up too high.
```

**What's wrong:**
- Inspirational immediately undercut by caution
- Empowerment mixed with defeatism
- Net effect: DEMOTIVATING
- Reader feels confused and deflated

**Tony Robbins Test:** Would Tony Robbins say this? Does it pump you up consistently or confuse your emotional state?

**Example of success:**
```
You can achieve anything you commit to with consistent, focused action. Yes, the path 
will have obstaclesâ€”that's how you know you're doing something significant. Most people 
quit when it gets hard, but you're not most people. You have everything you need to 
succeed. Dream big, take massive action, and keep pushing when others stop. You are 
unstoppable.
```

**What works:**
- Consistent empowerment message
- Obstacles reframed as validation, not threats
- Sustained emotional energy
- Reader knows how to feel: FIRED UP

**What the tool checks:**
- Emotional consistency
- Whether caution undermines inspiration
- Defeatist language mixed with empowerment
- Net emotional effect
- Whether reader has clear emotional direction

---

### Type 7: Auto-Detect
**Definition:** System analyzes the text and determines which type(s) of coherence it's attempting to achieve, then routes to appropriate analysis.

**How it works:**
The tool examines:
- Discourse markers (therefore, however, because = logical)
- Domain vocabulary (scientific terms = explanatory)
- Imperative verbs (do this, avoid that = instructional)
- Emotional language (you can, unstoppable = motivational)
- Narrative/sensory details (imagery, mood = thematic)

Then selects the most appropriate coherence type(s) and explains the choice.

## Technical Implementation Notes

### Core Algorithm for Each Coherence Type:

#### Type 1: Logical Consistency
```
1. Parse text into individual claims
2. Convert claims to logical propositions
3. Check for direct contradictions (X and Â¬X)
4. Check for incompatible predicates (red and blue simultaneously)
5. Flag all contradictions with line numbers
6. Generate rewrite that resolves contradictions
```

#### Type 2: Logical Cohesiveness
```
1. Identify claims, premises, conclusions
2. Build argument graph (what supports what)
3. Check for:
   - Unsupported conclusions
   - Missing intermediate steps
   - Non-sequiturs (claim doesn't follow)
   - Orphaned claims (not connected to main argument)
4. Score based on density of logical connections
5. Generate rewrite that adds missing support/removes orphans
```

#### Type 3: Scientific/Explanatory Coherence
```
1. Extract causal claims and mechanisms
2. Check against known physical laws (use knowledge base)
3. Identify:
   - Violations of conservation laws
   - Implausible mechanisms
   - Missing causal steps
   - Scale mismatches (quantum effects at macro scale)
4. Flag each violation with explanation
5. Generate rewrite using established scientific principles
```

#### Type 4: Thematic/Psychological Cohesiveness
```
1. Analyze emotional trajectory (sentiment over time)
2. Extract imagery and sensory details
3. Check for:
   - Emotional whiplash (rapid unm
otivated shifts)
   - Imagery-mood mismatch
   - Psychologically implausible sequences
4. Evaluate whether imagery supports emotional state
5. Generate rewrite that maintains emotional through-line
```

#### Type 5: Instructional Coherence
```
1. Extract all imperative statements (directives)
2. Categorize directives by goal/strategy
3. Check for:
   - Contradictory instructions
   - Conflicting priorities
   - Ambiguous action items
4. Build directive graph (what conflicts with what)
5. Generate rewrite with unified strategy, consistent directives
```

#### Type 6: Motivational Coherence
```
1. Analyze emotional valence of each sentence
2. Track empowerment vs deflation language
3. Check for:
   - Inspiration immediately undercut by caution
   - Empowerment mixed with defeatism
   - Confusing emotional direction
4. Calculate net motivational effect
5. Apply "Tony Robbins Test": Would this sustain high energy?
6. Generate rewrite that maintains consistent emotional direction
```

#### Type 7: Auto-Detect
```
1. Analyze discourse markers:
   - therefore, because, hence â†’ Logical
   - First, then, next â†’ Instructional
   - You can, unstoppable, achieve â†’ Motivational
   - Sensory details, mood words â†’ Thematic
   - Scientific terminology â†’ Explanatory

2. Check domain vocabulary
3. Count imperative vs declarative sentences
4. Analyze emotional language density
5. Select most appropriate type(s)
6. Explain reasoning for selection
7. Route to that coherence analysis
```

## Success Criteria

The Coherence Meter successfully analyzes text when it:

1. **Accurately identifies coherence failures** at the selected level
2. **Provides specific, actionable feedback** with line numbers and examples
3. **Generates rewrites that genuinely improve coherence** without losing content
4. **Explains changes clearly** so users understand what was improved
5. **Matches intuitive human judgment** about what "holds together"

The tool fails when it:
- Marks coherent text as incoherent
- Misidentifies the type of coherence needed
- Generates rewrites that lose important content
- Provides vague feedback without specific examples
- Creates rewrites that are technically correct but stilted/unnatural
